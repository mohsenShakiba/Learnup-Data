Handling Workplace Feedback
defensive, phrasing, blind spot, rapport, observable, privately, criticism, encouragement, intact, concrete, facilitator, follow-up, phrase, reaction, trigger, respectfully, accountability, habit, behavior, intention
I need to give Sam feedback, but he gets defensive.
Choose your phrasing carefully.
Should I do it privately?
Yes. Start with encouragement so the rapport stays intact.
Then mention the behavior?
Exactly. Make it observable and concrete.
His reaction can be strong when criticism hits a trigger.
Use one neutral phrase and pause.
Maybe it's a blind spot, not bad intention.
That helps you say it respectfully.
Should I involve a facilitator?
Only if the follow-up fails. The goal is accountability and a better habit.
