# Modern / Realist Public-Domain Stories for English Learners

16 stories of realist, slice-of-life fiction — everyday people, cities, jobs,
money, marriage, class — rather than fables or fairy tales. All are public
domain (authors died 70+ years ago; texts from Project Gutenberg). Each `.txt`
has a metadata header, and `index.csv` lists everything for your pipeline.

## Folders
- `b1/` — intermediate: mostly ironic/comic urban tales (7 stories)
- `b2/` — upper-intermediate: psychological & literary realism (9 stories)

## The "modern" ceiling
Copyright is the limit: public-domain fiction is essentially pre-1929. So the
most modern *and* legally free option is early-20th-century realism. These read
far less "once upon a time" than fairy tales — Joyce, Mansfield, Chekhov, and
O. Henry write about recognizable modern life — but the English is still
early-1900s. For contemporary settings/slang you'd need to license modern work
or write original stories (a good fit for your AI-generated content, since those
you own outright).

## Highlights by flavor
- Comic / twist endings (B1): O. Henry — The Cop and the Anthem, The Ransom of
  Red Chief, A Retrieved Reformation, Mammon and the Archer.
- Quiet human realism: Chekhov — Misery, The Schoolmistress, The Beauties;
  Maupassant — The Umbrella, The Piece of String.
- Psychological / literary (B2): Joyce — Eveline, Araby; Mansfield — Miss Brill,
  The Garden Party; Cather — Paul's Case; Chopin — Désirée's Baby, A Respectable
  Woman.

Levels are approximate — these are original texts, not graded readers, so plan
to simplify for true A2/B1 targets.
