# Public-Domain Short Stories for English Learners

23 complete stories, cleaned of Project Gutenberg boilerplate and organized by
approximate CEFR level. Each `.txt` file has a metadata header (title, author,
year, level, word count, source) followed by the story text.

## Folders
- `a1-a2/` — beginner: fables & simple tales (8 stories)
- `b1/`    — intermediate: classic short stories (10 stories)
- `b2/`    — upper-intermediate: richer vocabulary & syntax (5 stories)

`index.csv` lists every story with level, author, year, and word count — handy
for feeding into a content pipeline.

## Licensing
All texts are in the public domain, sourced from Project Gutenberg via GitHub
mirrors. Rule of thumb used: original authors died 70+ years ago, and every
translation chosen (Aesop – Vernon Jones 1912; Grimm – Margaret Hunt; Tolstoy –
Louise & Aylmer Maude; Chekhov – Constance Garnett; Maupassant – public-domain
edition) is itself out of copyright. You are free to read, adapt, re-level,
re-voice (e.g. with Kokoro TTS), and redistribute them.

Note: the CEFR levels are *approximate* — they reflect the original texts, which
were not written for learners. For true graded difficulty you'll likely want to
simplify the B1/B2 pieces down to your target level.

## Substitution note
Andersen's #1597 collection does not contain "The Ugly Duckling," so this set
uses "The Emperor's New Clothes" and "The Little Match Girl" instead. Kate
Chopin's "The Story of an Hour" was not in the available public-domain
collection, so "A Pair of Silk Stockings" (same author, similar level) is
included in its place.
