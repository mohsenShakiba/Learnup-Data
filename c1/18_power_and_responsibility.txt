Power and Responsibility
power, corrupt, temptation, restraint, abuse, wield, principled, power dynamic, admirable, authority abuse, concentration of power, legitimacy, ethical restraint, checks and balances, public interest, accountability gap, institutional control, unchecked power, moral authority, coercion
They say power corrupts. Do you agree?
It reveals character more than it corrupts it.
So weakness was always there?
Often, yes. A power dynamic simply removes restraint.
Then accountability becomes essential.
Without scrutiny, the temptation grows.
Yet some wield power with real integrity.
Those are the admirable ones with moral authority.
It takes remarkable humility.
And a clear sense of public interest, legitimacy, and responsibility.
Perhaps the real test is ethical restraint under unchecked power.
Indeed. How you use power defines you.
Without checks and balances, institutional control can become coercion.
That is when authority abuse, concentration of power, and the accountability gap become dangerous.
