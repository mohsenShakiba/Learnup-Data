The Meaning of Home
nostalgia-pull, hearth, homeland, uprooted, familiarity, sanctuary, dwelling, kinship, settledness, homecoming, groundedness, refuge, ancestral, transient, place-attachment, nesting, homeliness, expatriate, longing, homestead
After years abroad as an expatriate, what does home even mean to you?
Less a dwelling, more a feeling of sanctuary and refuge.
So home is the familiarity, not the address?
Yes — the groundedness and settledness a place gives you.
I feel that nostalgia-pull whenever I picture my ancestral homeland.
The old homestead, the hearth, the smell of the kitchen.
For me it's kinship — home is wherever my people are.
A homecoming, then, is really a return to belonging.
Though my life now feels transient, always moving.
That constant nesting must weaken your place-attachment.
It does. I ache with a quiet longing for permanence.
Perhaps home is something you carry, not somewhere you're uprooted from.
Maybe true homeliness is a state of mind.
