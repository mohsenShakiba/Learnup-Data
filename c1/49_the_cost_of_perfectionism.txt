The Cost of Perfectionism
perfectionism, flawless, impossibly high, all-or-nothing, self criticism, good-enough, nitpick, paralysis by perfection, unrelenting, standards too high, never satisfied, overpolish, done is better, fear of mistakes, harsh inner critic, diminishing returns, self flagellation, tweak endlessly, imperfection, exacting
My perfectionism is starting to cost me everything.
How so? A drive for high quality isn't bad in itself.
But I chase the flawless, the impossibly high, and never finish.
That's paralysis by perfection. Your standards too high freeze you.
I'll tweak endlessly, overpolish one paragraph for hours.
Classic diminishing returns. At some point good-enough really is enough.
But "good enough" feels like failure to me.
That's the all-or-nothing trap, and the harsh inner critic feeding it.
The self criticism is relentless — pure self flagellation.
Because you're never satisfied, no matter how well it goes.
And I nitpick every tiny imperfection.
A fear of mistakes so unrelenting it stops you from shipping.
So I should accept being less exacting?
Remember: done is better than perfect. Progress beats polish.
