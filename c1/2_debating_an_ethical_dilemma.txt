Debating an Ethical Dilemma
principle, intention, integrity, rationalize, nuance, subjective, moral, consequences, morality, consequentialism, deontology, relativism, absolutism, impartiality, harm, fairness, remorse, justification, outcome, motive
Would you ever bend the rules for a good cause?
It depends. The intention and motive matter, but so do the consequences.
So you think the end can justify the means?
Not always. Pure consequentialism can become a slippery slope.
Once you compromise your principles, where do you stop?
Precisely. It's easy to rationalize small steps with a neat justification.
Yet life rarely offers black-and-white choices.
That's the nuance deontology and absolutism sometimes overlook.
So morality is partly subjective?
To a degree, but relativism cannot excuse real harm.
I suppose we each have to weigh the outcome with impartiality.
And with fairness, so remorse arrives before the damage is done.
