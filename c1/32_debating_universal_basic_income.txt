Debating Universal Basic Income
stipend, safety-net, redistribution, means-tested, disincentive, poverty-line, welfare-trap, affordability, taxpayer-funded, subsistence, purchasing-power, pilot-program, guaranteed-income, work-ethic, inflationary, fiscal, cost-neutral, universality, decommodify, paternalism
A universal stipend could finally lift everyone above the poverty-line.
But is a guaranteed-income really within reach? I worry about affordability.
Proponents argue it could be nearly cost-neutral if we simplify welfare.
How? A taxpayer-funded safety-net for all sounds fiscally reckless.
Through redistribution and scrapping dozens of means-tested programs.
Yet that testing at least targets those at subsistence level.
It also creates a welfare-trap — a disincentive to work.
You think universality removes that?
Exactly. No clawback, so it protects your work-ethic and purchasing-power.
Won't handing out cash be inflationary?
A well-designed pilot-program can test the fiscal effects first.
I still fear it, but I admit it might decommodify basic dignity.
And end the paternalism of telling the poor how to live.
