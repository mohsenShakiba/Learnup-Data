Debating Censorship

Where do you draw the line on censorship?
I lean toward near-absolute free-speech, even for offensive views.
But unchecked speech enables propaganda and real harm.
So you'd trust some authority with editorial-control over ideas?
Content moderation isn't the same as state suppression.
It can be. Deplatforming a dissenting voice still silences dissent.
Only incitement and direct threats should cross the line.
Agreed there — but who defines incitement? That's the slippery-slope.
A watchdog press exists precisely to resist that overreach.
And yet gatekeeping by platforms creates its own chilling-effect.
People then practice self-censorship to avoid trouble.
Which is why I defend the marketplace-of-ideas over any urge to sanitize.
Still, should we let anyone redact a whistleblower or ban protected-speech?
No. That's exactly the taboo we must guard against.
