Individualism and Collectivism
individualism, collectivism, communal, society, interdependent, cohesion, liberty, cultural, equilibrium, individual liberty, common good, social fabric, mutual obligation, collective duty, autonomy rights, community minded, cultural balance, self reliance, social contract, shared responsibility
Western culture really prizes individualism.
While many other societies are more communal.
Each has clear strengths and weaknesses.
Individualism fosters innovation, liberty, and self reliance.
But it can erode social cohesion and the social fabric.
Whereas collectivism strengthens belonging.
Yet it may suppress personal autonomy and autonomy rights.
So there's an inherent tension between them.
Is one objectively better, though?
I doubt it. Context shapes what works.
Perhaps the healthiest societies find cultural balance and equilibrium.
Balancing individual liberty with the common good, mutual obligation, and collective duty.
That is the social contract of a community minded society with shared responsibility.
