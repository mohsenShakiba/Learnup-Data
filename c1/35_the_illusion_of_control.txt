The Illusion of Control
control-illusion, surrender, unpredictability, letting-go, powerlessness, chaos, overplanning, agency-limits, happenstance, relinquish, grip, controllables, equanimity, uncontrollable, fixation, yield, flux, stoic, illusory, outcome-fixation
I plan everything meticulously, yet life keeps surprising me.
That's the control-illusion — the sense that a tight grip guarantees outcomes.
So all my overplanning is basically illusory?
Partly. You can manage the controllables, but not the uncontrollable.
It's hard to accept that much unpredictability.
Recognizing your agency-limits is the first step toward equanimity.
You're saying I should just surrender to chaos?
Not surrender to it — make peace with the flux.
That sounds very stoic.
It is. The Stoics urged us to relinquish what we can't command.
My fixation on results, this outcome-fixation, exhausts me.
Then practice letting-go. Yield where forcing won't help.
And admit that so much is just happenstance.
Exactly. Accepting powerlessness, ironically, gives you back some power.
