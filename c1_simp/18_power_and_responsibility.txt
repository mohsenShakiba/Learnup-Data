Power and Responsibility
power, corrupt, temptation, restraint, abuse, wield, principled
They say power corrupts. Do you agree?
It reveals character more than it corrupts it.
So weakness was always there?
Often, yes. Power simply removes restraint.
Then accountability becomes essential.
Without scrutiny, the temptation grows.
Yet some wield power with real integrity.
Those are the ones worth admiring.
It takes remarkable humility.
And a clear sense of responsibility.
Perhaps the real test is restraint.
Indeed. How you use power defines you.
