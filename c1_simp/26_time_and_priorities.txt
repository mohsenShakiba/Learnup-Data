Time and Priorities
finite, intentional, squander, deliberate, scarce, time-management, opportunity-cost, regret-test, life-drift, essentialism, priority-setting, finite-resource, intentional-living, no-by-default, attention-budget, urgent-important, time-blocking, calendar-discipline, deep-work, life-priority
We treat time as if it were limitless.
Yet it's our scarcest resource.
We squander it on trivial things and poor time-management.
Because we rarely pause to reflect.
How do you decide what truly matters?
I ask a regret-test: what would I regret neglecting?
That's a sobering way to choose priority-setting.
Every yes has an opportunity-cost.
So priorities are really trade-offs and an attention-budget.
Exactly. We must be intentional and sometimes no-by-default.
Otherwise life-drift takes over, even with urgent-important tasks.
Living deliberately means essentialism, intentional-living, time-blocking, calendar-discipline, deep-work, and respecting life-priority as a finite-resource.
