How do you actually define success?
Not by the conventional metrics anymore.
You mean wealth and status?
Yes. Those are largely external validation and an achievement-metric.
But society constantly pushes those yardsticks.
Which makes them feel oddly arbitrary.
So success is deeply subjective?
It has to be. Each life is different.
I've chased external goals, prestige-chasing, and status-anxiety for years.
And did they bring lasting fulfilment?
Honestly, no. They created a fulfilment-gap and hollow-success.
Then perhaps it's time for redefinition, inner-validation, and a new success-script.
One built around self-worth, enoughness, personal-definition, self-defined-success, and deliberate life-design.
