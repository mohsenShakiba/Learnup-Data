Mentorship and Growth
mentee, sounding board, role model, feedback loop, blind spot, tough love, wisdom transfer, championing, professional growth, hands off, stretch assignment, pay it forward, unlock potential, seasoned, protege, apprentice, mentoring, guiding hand, steer, invest in people
You've been mentoring juniors for years. What makes it work?
Treating each mentee as a protege, not just an apprentice ticking boxes.
So it's more than a feedback loop on their tasks?
Far more. A good mentor is a sounding board and a role model at once.
Do you prefer a hands off style or a firmer guiding hand?
Depends on the person. Sometimes they need tough love to grow.
How do you help them see their weaknesses?
I name the blind spot gently, then give a stretch assignment to work on it.
That's real professional growth, not just praise.
Exactly. My job is to unlock potential and steer without controlling.
Where does the wisdom transfer really happen?
In small moments — a seasoned hand championing someone's first big idea.
Why invest in people so heavily?
Because someone once did it for me. Now I pay it forward.
