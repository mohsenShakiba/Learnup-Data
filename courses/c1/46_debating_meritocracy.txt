Debating Meritocracy
meritocracy, level playing field, privilege, social mobility, head start, luck versus effort, equal opportunity, systemic, bootstraps, inherited advantage, self-made, fairness myth, upward mobility, talent and grit, unequal start, rigged, class ceiling, birth lottery, deservingness, effort blind
Do you really believe we live in a meritocracy?
Ideally, effort and talent and grit should determine success.
But that assumes a level playing field that doesn't exist.
You mean privilege and a head start skew the outcomes?
Exactly. Some are born with inherited advantage; others face an unequal start.
So the "self-made" story is partly a fairness myth?
Often, yes. It's really luck versus effort, and we ignore the luck.
Still, hard work does drive social mobility for some.
For some — but systemic barriers create a real class ceiling.
You think the game is rigged, then?
Not entirely, but "pull yourself up by your bootstraps" ignores the birth lottery.
So true equal opportunity would mean upward mobility for anyone.
Right. Judging deservingness while being effort blind to circumstances is unjust.
Fair enough. Merit matters, but it isn't the whole story.
