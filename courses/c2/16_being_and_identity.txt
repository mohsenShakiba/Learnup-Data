Being and Identity
being and identity, selfhood, continuity, consciousness, embodiment, recognition, authenticity, social identity, narrative self, alienation, autonomy, memory, personhood, existential question, becoming, relational identity, inner life, external label, belonging, self-understanding
I used to think identity was something you discovered once and kept.
Now you see it as a process of becoming?
Yes, but continuity still matters. Otherwise selfhood dissolves into mood.
Memory gives continuity, but recognition gives it a social form.
That is where external labels can become suffocating.
Especially when they deny the complexity of someone's inner life.
Can authenticity exist without belonging?
Perhaps, but it becomes lonely very quickly.
Embodiment also matters. We are not just stories we tell about ourselves.
True. Personhood is lived through bodies, relationships, and choices.
So identity is both autonomous and relational.
Exactly. The existential question is not simply who I am, but how I become myself with others.
