The budget forces a choice between efficiency and distributive fairness.
Scarcity always does, but the criteria should be transparent.
Do we prioritize need, merit, or existing entitlement?
That depends on the service and the moral claim behind it.
In healthcare, vulnerability should carry special weight.
In education, equity may require giving more to those who start with less.
Some taxpayers will call that unfair burden sharing.
Then procedural justice matters. People need to see how the decision was made.
Rationing sounds harsh, but pretending resources are unlimited is worse.
Agreed. Hidden rationing usually protects the already powerful.
So our allocation model should name the trade-off openly.
