Regulating Innovation

If regulation arrives too late, the market will set the norms for us.
But if it arrives too early, it may freeze useful innovation.
That is why an adaptive regulation model makes sense.
A regulatory sandbox could allow experimentation with clear boundaries.
The company wants flexibility because the technology is still evolving.
Consumers want protection because the risks are already real.
Legal uncertainty also favors large firms that can absorb compliance costs.
So poorly designed rules may become a barrier to entry.
We need evidence gathering before permanent obligations are imposed.
Yes, but precaution should guide areas involving public safety.
Policy lag is not neutral; it distributes risk quietly.
Regulating innovation means learning fast without abandoning accountability.
