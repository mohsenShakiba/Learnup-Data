Emotion and Judgment
emotion and judgment, affect, intuition, rationality, emotional intelligence, fear response, moral judgment, empathy, bias, decision making, anger, compassion, self-regulation, perception, deliberation, impulse, cognitive appraisal, emotional evidence, credibility, reflective distance
I do not want to pretend emotion has no place in judgment.
It often tells us what deserves attention.
Fear can reveal risk, but it can also exaggerate it.
Exactly. The fear response needs deliberation, not dismissal.
Anger may point to injustice.
And still distort perception if it becomes the whole argument.
So emotional intelligence is not emotional suppression.
It is self-regulation and reflective distance.
Can empathy be a kind of evidence?
It can guide moral judgment, but it needs cognitive appraisal.
Compassion without rationality may become impulsive.
And rationality without affect may miss what human beings actually value.
