Learning Big Numbers

Today we'll practice the big numbers. Can you count to a hundred for me?
I can try. It's easy up to twenty, then my head starts to hurt.
Take it slowly. After a hundred comes a thousand, and after that, a million.
A million! That's the total number of people in a small city, isn't it?
More or less. Half a million would be a large town. Now, what is ten times ten?
A hundred. And a dozen is twelve — I remember that one.
Good. Zero is the easiest of all. Now it's your turn — say them all twice for practice.
One, two, three, all the way up. My daughter is young, but she learns numbers fast.
Children born today grow up counting in the millions. The world is full of big numbers.
True. We even bought theatre seats numbered five hundred, right over on the west side.
