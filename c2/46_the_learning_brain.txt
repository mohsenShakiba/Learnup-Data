The Learning Brain

Students often reread notes because it feels productive.
But retrieval practice usually produces more durable learning.
The learning brain needs effort, not just exposure.
And sleep supports memory consolidation more than people realize.
Cognitive load is a problem when the lesson asks too much at once.
Prior knowledge reduces that load and helps transfer.
What about motivation?
It matters, but it cannot replace a sound learning strategy.
Feedback should target misconceptions quickly.
And spaced repetition prevents confidence from fading into forgetting.
Metacognition helps learners judge what they actually know.
Neuroplasticity is real, but it rewards consistent practice, not wishful thinking.
