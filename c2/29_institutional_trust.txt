Public confidence collapsed because the failure confirmed what people already suspected.
A trust deficit rarely begins with one incident.
It grows when communication is vague and accountability is delayed.
And when service delivery feels arbitrary.
The agency wants a transparency campaign.
Transparency helps only if it exposes something real.
Otherwise it becomes another credibility problem.
Oversight must be independent, and participation cannot be tokenistic.
Do you think competence matters more than integrity?
They reinforce each other. Corruption wastes competence, and incompetence weakens integrity.
So repair requires consistent fairness, not a single apology.
Institutional trust is rebuilt through culture, governance, and repeated proof.
