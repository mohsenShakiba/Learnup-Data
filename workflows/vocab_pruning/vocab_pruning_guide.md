# Vocab Pruning Guide

How to prune a vocab list (e.g. `missing_a1_vocabs.csv`) down to a clean set of
high-frequency, teachable everyday/work words. The goal is the **middle band**:
drop words that are too niche/specific *and* words that are too basic/trivial,
keeping the mid-frequency vocabulary that's actually worth teaching.

File format is `NUMBER<TAB>WORD`, one per line. After pruning, renumber
sequentially from 1.

## The core principle

Keep a word only if it is **both**:

1. **High-frequency in general everyday or work English** — not tied to one
   narrow topic (cooking, a specific sport, fantasy stories), and
2. **Worth a vocab card** — not so basic that any learner already knows it
   (pronouns, articles, greetings), and not so trivial/ambiguous that it
   teaches nothing on its own.

When in doubt, **cut it**. Shrinking the list is progress. A shorter list of
genuinely useful words beats a padded one.

## REMOVE — too niche / specific / low-utility

Words that only matter inside one narrow topic. We're learning general English,
not a domain vocabulary.

- **Specific animals** — snake, lion, monkey, frog, tiger, cow, pig, bee, bat,
  bird, fish. (Keep the general word `animal`.)
- **Specific foods & drinks** — tomato, banana, egg, soup, sandwich, cookie,
  candy, chocolate, ice cream, juice, cream, meat, potato. (Keep neutral
  containers: `cup`, `glass`, `bottle`, `meal`.)
- **Named sports** — basketball, baseball, volleyball, tennis, soccer, football,
  swimming. (Keep general `game`, `sport`, `player`.)
- **Instruments & obsolete media** — piano, drum, album, CD, DVD, radio,
  television/TV, cartoon.
- **Toys / fantasy / story words** — doll, kite, bell, prince, princess, king,
  palace, ghost, fairy.
- **Niche objects / one-off nouns** — apron, vase, bucket, ribbon, ruler,
  towel, wheel, hole, flag, jewelry, haircut.
- **Named places / proper-noun-ish** — zoo, temple, Olympics, opera.
- **Minor body parts** — knee, shoulder, bone, brain. (Keep major: eye, ear,
  nose, hand, arm, leg, face.)

## REMOVE — too basic / trivial (not worth a card)

Words every learner already knows or that are pure grammar, not vocabulary.

- **Pronouns & possessives** — I, you, he, she, it, we, they, me, him, her,
  my, your, his, their, this, that, these, those, mine, yours.
- **Question words & determiners** — who, what, why, which, how, when, whose,
  each, every, both, any, some, all, much, many, more, most, other.
- **Articles / basic conjunctions / basic prepositions** — a, an, the, and,
  or, but, so, as, than, to, of, for, on, at, by, up, out, down, over, into,
  with, about, after, before, here, there, **not**.
- **Be / auxiliaries / modals** — is, am, are, was, be, have, has, do, does,
  did, can, will, would, could, must, may, `'m`, `'re`, `'s`.
- **Trivial adverbs** — very, too, just, now, then, well, also, never, always,
  often, already, really, only, sure.
- **Trivial verbs** — go, get, make, take, want, tell, say, look, give, let.
- **Greetings / social formulas** — hello, yes, no, bye, please, sir, pardon,
  good morning/afternoon/night, OK, all right, Mr., Mrs.

## REMOVE — ambiguous, duplicate, or app-meta

- **Casual duplicates** — mom/mum/dad/daddy/grandma/grandpa (keep the standard
  mother/father/grandmother/grandfather); couch vs sofa; mobile vs mobile
  phone; trousers vs pants. Keep one form.
- **Too ambiguous to teach as one word** — set, own, same, fan, board, block,
  bar, pop, blow, case.
- **App/instruction-only words** — underline (not everyday conversation).

## KEEP — the middle band (examples)

- **Everyday places** — store, hospital, college, church, supermarket, bridge,
  farm, subway, bookstore, cafe, hotel, bank.
- **Neutral objects** — bottle, glass, cup, coat, dress, shoe, pen, pencil,
  bag, clock, camera, notebook.
- **Useful verbs** — practice, celebrate, forget, breathe, climb, listen,
  travel, repeat, guess, win, hold.
- **Descriptive adjectives** — strange, beautiful, terrible, wonderful, lonely,
  handsome, clever, rude, difficult, famous.
- **People / family / roles** — grandfather, uncle, aunt, cousin, husband,
  wife, neighbor, doctor, nurse, engineer, farmer.
- **Weather / nature** — sky, sun, moon, cloud, rainy, cloudy, snowy, wind,
  tree, flower, sea, mountain.
- **Profile / practical** — nationality, last name, age, health, business.
- **Time notation** — o'clock, a.m., p.m.

## Deliberately kept (borderline, but they earn it)

- **Numbers, months, days** — basic, but genuine standalone vocabulary, not
  function words. Keep unless you specifically want them thinned.
- **Teachable connectors** — because, until. (But cut the trivial ones: and,
  or, but, so, if.)
- **Reflexives / phrasal** — yourself, each other, have to.

## Quick repeat checklist

For each remaining word, ask in order — a "yes" to any means **cut**:

1. Is it locked to one narrow topic (a specific animal, food, sport,
   instrument, toy, story)?
2. Is it a pronoun, article, basic preposition/conjunction, auxiliary/modal,
   or greeting?
3. Is it a trivial verb/adverb every beginner already knows?
4. Is it a casual duplicate of a word already on the list?
5. Is it too ambiguous or meta to teach as a single card?

If none apply and it's a word people genuinely use in everyday/work
conversation — **keep it**.
